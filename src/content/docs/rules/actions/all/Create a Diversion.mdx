<Action title={"Create a Diversion"} traits={[['Mental']]} action={2} level={0} url={"https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2387"}>
 **Source:** Player Core pg. 237

  With a gesture, a trick, or some distracting words, you can create a diversion that draws creatures' attention elsewhere. If you use a gesture or trick, this action gains the [manipulate](/Traits.aspx?ID=104) trait. If you use distracting words, it gains the [auditory](/Traits.aspx?ID=16) and [linguistic](/Traits.aspx?ID=101) traits.

 Attempt a single [Deception](/Skills.aspx?ID=5) check and compare it to the Perception DCs of the creatures whose attention you're trying to divert. Whether or not you succeed, creatures you attempt to divert gain a +4 circumstance bonus to their Perception DCs against your attempts to Create a Diversion for 1 minute.

**Success** You become [hidden](/Conditions.aspx?ID=22) to each creature whose Perception DC is less than or equal to your result. (The hidden condition allows you to [Sneak](/Actions.aspx?ID=63) away) This lasts until the end of your turn or until you do anything except Step or use the [Stealth](/Skills.aspx?ID=15) skill to [Hide](/Actions.aspx?ID=62) or Sneak. If you Strike a creature, the creature remains [off-guard](/Conditions.aspx?ID=58) against that attack, and you then become [observed](/Conditions.aspx?ID=27). If you do anything else, you become observed just before you act unless the GM determines otherwise.<br />
**Failure** You don't divert the attention of any creatures whose Perception DC exceeds your result, and those creatures are aware you were trying to trick them.

 </Action>
