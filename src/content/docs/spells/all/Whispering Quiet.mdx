<Spell title={"Whispering Quiet"} traits={[['Uncommon', 3], ['Cleric'], ['Focus'], ['Manipulate'], ['Sonic']]} action={2} pfs={"Standard"} level={1} url={"https://2e.aonprd.com/Spells.aspx?ID=1834"} area={"15-foot burst"} domain={['Secrecy']} duration={60} legacy_name={"Forced Quiet"} range={60} source={['Player Core pg. 379']} spell_type={"Focus"} type={"Spell"}>
You suppress sound in an area, preventing anyone from giving away valuable secrets. This doesn't prevent those in the area from talking or Casting a Spell, but no creature more than 5 feet away can hear its voice without succeeding at a Perception check against your spell DC, which might interfere with [auditory](/Traits.aspx?ID=16) or [linguistic](/Traits.aspx?ID=101) effects as well as communication. Other sounds created in the area are muffled to a similar volume unless that sound is created by an effect with the [sonic](/Traits.aspx?ID=147) trait.

</Spell>
