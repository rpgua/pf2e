You transform the target into an [undead](/Traits.aspx?ID=160) creature with a level up to that allowed in the Creature Creation Rituals table. There are many versions of this ritual, each specific to a particular type of undead (one ritual for all [zombies](/MonsterFamilies.aspx?ID=103), one for [skeletons](/MonsterFamilies.aspx?ID=92), one for [ghouls](/MonsterFamilies.aspx?ID=52), and so on), and the rituals that create [rare](/Traits.aspx?ID=137) undead are also rare. Some forms of undead, such as [liches](/Monsters.aspx?ID=282), form using their own unique methods and can't be created with a version of _create undead_.

**Critical Success** The target becomes an undead creature of the appropriate type. If it's at least 4 levels lower than you, you can make it a minion. This gives it the [minion](/Traits.aspx?ID=109) trait, meaning it can use 2 actions when you command it, and commanding it is a single action that has the [auditory](/Traits.aspx?ID=16) and [concentrate](/Actions.aspx?ID=683) traits. You can have a maximum of four minions under your control. If it's intelligent and doesn't become a minion, the undead is [helpful](/Conditions.aspx?ID=21) to you for awakening it, though it's still a horrid and evil creature. If it's unintelligent and doesn't become a minion, you can give it one simple command. It pursues that goal single-mindedly, ignoring any of your subsequent commands.<br />
**Success** As critical success, except an intelligent undead that doesn't become your minion is only [friendly](/Conditions.aspx?ID=18) to you, and an unintelligent undead that doesn't become your minion leaves you alone unless you attack it. It marauds the local area rather than following your command.<br />
**Failure** You fail to create the undead.<br />
**Critical Failure** You create the undead, but its soul, tortured by your foul necromancy, is full of nothing but hatred for you. It attempts to destroy you.
