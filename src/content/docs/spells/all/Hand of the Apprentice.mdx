<Spell title={"Hand of the Apprentice"} traits={[['Uncommon', 3], ['Attack'], ['Focus'], ['Manipulate'], ['Wizard']]} action={1} pfs={"Standard"} level={1} url={"https://2e.aonprd.com/Spells.aspx?ID=1906"} range={500} saving_throw={"AC"} source={['Player Core pg. 388']} spell_type={"Focus"} target={"1 creature"} type={"Spell"}>
You take advantage of one of the most fundamental lessons of magic to levitate and propel your weapon. You hurl a held melee weapon with which you are trained at the target, making a spell attack roll. On a success, you deal the weapon's damage as if you had hit with a melee Strike, but add your spellcasting attribute modifier to damage, rather than your Strength modifier. On a critical success, you deal double damage, and you add the weapon's [critical specialization effect](/Rules.aspx?ID=235). Regardless of the outcome, the weapon flies back to you and returns to your hand.

</Spell>
