<Spell title={"Ephemeral Tracking"} traits={[['Uncommon', 3], ['Concentrate'], ['Focus'], ['Manipulate'], ['Ranger']]} action={2} pfs={"Standard"} level={3} url={"https://2e.aonprd.com/Spells.aspx?ID=1870"} duration={3600} source={['Player Core pg. 384']} spell_type={"Focus"} type={"Spell"}>
You can look at currents in the air and water and see tracks that you can follow just as you would on land. You can use [Survival](/Skills.aspx?ID=16) to [Track](/Actions.aspx?ID=66) through both air and water, noticing aerial or aquatic “tracks” of all creatures who passed nearby in the last hour. It's more difficult to Track in this way: the DC to Track through air or water is always at least 30, or higher after precipitation or wind (in the air) or heavy tides or currents (in the water). It's possible to [Cover Tracks](/Actions.aspx?ID=65) against this spell, but creatures might not realize they need to do so.

</Spell>
