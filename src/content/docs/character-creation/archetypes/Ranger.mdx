You have studied hunting, tracking, and wilderness survival, adding a ranger’s tools to your skill set. Your keen eyes while hunting prey help gather more information.

<title level="3">Multiclass Ranger characters</title>

The ranger archetype has access to excellent options to improve a character’s monster knowledge and survival skills, but ranger is of particular interest to any character wanting to become a dedicated archer. 
<ul><li>[Bard](/Classes.aspx?ID=32) rangers support their allies and animal companions with expanded combat options. The bard’s knowledge can also complement the ranger’s investigation of their foes.</li><li>[Cleric](/Classes.aspx?ID=33) rangers are particularly fitting for deities who connect thematically to nature or have a bow as their favored weapon. (Or, in the case of [Erastil](/Deities.aspx?ID=6), both!)</li><li>[Druids](/Classes.aspx?ID=34) have the most thematic overlap with rangers among the spellcasters, allowing you to create a true master of the wilderness.</li><li>[Fighter](/Classes.aspx?ID=35) rangers are among the most fearsome archers, combining the feats from both classes to create a fighting style unique to each fighter ranger. Some warden spells can also be particularly helpful to a fighter.</li><li>[Rogue](/Classes.aspx?ID=37) rangers make excellent snipers or ranged skirmishers, with their rogue skills naturally playing into the ranger’s strengths.</li><li>[Witch](/Classes.aspx?ID=38) rangers add a new prowess scouting to the information they can gain through their familiar and patron.</li><li>[Wizard](/Classes.aspx?ID=39) rangers benefit greatly from ranger’s feat support, backing up their spells with ranged weapon attacks.</li></ul>
