<row gap="tiny">**Source** [Player Core](/Sources.aspx?ID=216) pg. 221</row>

You’ve learned to sneak, steal, and disable traps. With time and luck, you’ll become capable of moving through the shadows, striking unseen, and escaping without notice.

<title level="3">Multiclass Rogue characters</title>

Nearly any character can benefit from the many skills and tricks the rogue archetype grants. 
<ul><li>[Bard](/Classes.aspx?ID=32) rogues expand their robust base of skills. They are also well-suited to take advantage of the distractions caused by their performances and otherwise apply their skills to combat.</li><li>[Cleric](/Classes.aspx?ID=33) rogues often follow gods that support trickery in combat, such as [Calistria](/Deities.aspx?ID=3), learning a combat style that fits their god. However, many clerics might need stealth and trickery when their voices are unwelcome.</li><li>[Druid](/Classes.aspx?ID=34) rogues can ambush foes in other forms, seamlessly fading into the shadows of the natural world.</li><li>[Fighter](/Classes.aspx?ID=35) rogues combine the fighter’s accuracy with extra damage, a deadly combination for archers or finesse fighters.</li><li>[Ranger](/Classes.aspx?ID=36) rogues benefit from the shared focus on trailing foes and catching them off</li><li>guard. The [flurry](/HuntersEdge.aspx?ID=1) edge can get you many sneak attacks, and the [precision](/HuntersEdge.aspx?ID=2) edge doubles down on precision damage.</li><li>[Witch](/Classes.aspx?ID=38) rogues apply the hexes of their patrons from the shadows and improve many of the simple weapons they’re trained in.</li><li>[Wizard](/Classes.aspx?ID=39) rogues use the rogue multiclass to shore up skills or to pick up tricky rogue feats to help keep them safe.</li></ul>
